Arena Battle Robot — Mechanical & Mechatronic Design

2023 · Team Project · Mechatronics Competition

SolidWorks Chassis & Armor GD&T ESP32 · C++ Sensors & Actuators Perf-board Soldering Wiring Layout

Gallery

Overview

  • Built a battle-ready arena robot emphasizing mechanical robustness and serviceability.
  • Designed chassis and armor plates in SolidWorks; packaging for quick repairs.
  • Integrated sensors, actuators, and electronics within a protected frame and cable channels.
  • ESP32 (C++) control for drive and weapon modules; modular connectors for fast swap.

Mechanical Design

  • Modeled drive mounts, gear supports, battery bay, and top armor with GD&T on critical fits.
  • Balanced center of mass for traction and turning; kept wiring clearances and strain relief.
  • Mixed fabrication: laser cutting MDF plates + 3D-printed brackets for sensor guards.
  • Exploded assemblies, BOM, and shop drawings prepared for rapid machining and assembly.

Electronics Integration

  • Integrated TOF / limit sensors for proximity and boundary detection.
  • Controlled dual DC drive and weapon servo via ESP32 (C++) with PWM and GPIO interrupts.
  • Designed and hand-soldered a perf-board control module with labeled harnesses and fusing.
  • Planned wiring layout to separate power/signal paths and simplify field maintenance.

Testing & Results

  • Bench-tested drivetrain under load; revised gear ratio for improved acceleration.
  • Validated sensor reliability after impacts; connections held via strain-relieved harness.
  • Achieved 1st place out of 30+ participated teams.
  • Demonstrated full mechanical–electrical integration with quick swap/repair workflow.
